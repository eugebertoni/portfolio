Sharing

A fast solution for your sharing expenses

What is Sharing?

Is a solution application for organizing group expenses. Perfect for trips, holidays, meals or sharing flat expenses.

What makes Sharing unique?

The app do the calculations - and determine who owes what. The app finds the most efficient way for everyone to pay each other what they owe.

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Lead the product storyboarding and did light user research.
  • Did the User Experience Design including creating key user flows and wireframes.
  • Create high fidelity wireframes to show the full product experience.

Storyboard

A storyboard help us identify key moments in the experience of sharing expenses.

  • Listing expenses

    Bae, now that we live together we should split expenses.

  • Going to the supermarket

    Honey, I'll go to the supermarket.

  • Going to the hardware store

    Bae, I need to buy the spare part to fix the oven.

  • Checking balances

    Bae, how much have you spent? How much do I owe you?

  • Refund

    Honey I owe you $137. I'll make you a bank transfer.

The storyboard establish a vision with stakeholders and revealed issues and key screens early in the product development process.

How other participants can add expenses?
Would be mandatory to create a profile?
Can the user not be registered?

The storyboard forced discussion with stakeholders, beyond the specifics of each screen.
This helped narrow the app scope before team invested time and money into the build.

Based on the storyboard, we identified the main 2 participants in the story

Considerations
  • How one user uploads expenses vs. the other?
  • Is everybody going to share the expenses?

For each proto-character we identified the key tasks as well as their emotional state at each stage of the journey.

User flows for each key moment helped us identify key actions within the app and the screens to focus on first

User flows

I mapped out the users’ steps to see how I could simplify their journey to help them reach their most important goals with the product.

Low fidelity wireframes help to understand and experience the defined flows
