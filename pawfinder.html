PawFinder

An app to find your lost best friend

What is PawFinder?

A conceptual interactive prototype to help users find and be reunited with their lost pet.

What makes PawFinder unique?

The key constraint for this project was that the pet may not wear a tracking device, unlike many other tracking systems.

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Lead the product storyboarding and did light user research.
  • Did the User Experience Design including creating key user flows and wireframes.
  • Created high fidelity wireframes to show the full product experience.

Storyboard

A storyboard helps us identify key moments in the experience of a pet going missing and being found.

  • Enjoying the day

    It's so nice to spend the day with my dog!

  • Pet goes missing

    Oh no! Where did Toby go? I need help now!

  • Other users get alert

    There is a missing dog, help us find him.

  • Someone spots the pet

    That's the missing dog. I'll let the owner know.

  • Reuniting the owners

    Toby is OK! Stay where you are, I'll come to you.

  • Report a lost pet
  • Alert about lost pet
  • Report pet as found

The storyboard establishes a vision with stakeholders and revealed issues and key screens early in the product development process.

How will people know about the app?

Pet has no tracking device

The storyboard forced discussion with stakeholders, beyond the specifics of each screen.
This helped narrow the app scope before the team invested time and money into the build.

Based on the storyboard, we identified the main 3 participants in the story

Personas

We used proto-characters to help us stay anchored on the users and avoid letting our desire for features trump user needs.

  • Toby

    Lost Pet

  • Derek

    Pet Owner

  • Sue

    The user who spots the pet

Needs to accomplish...
  • Report pet as missing
  • Provide details about the animal
  • Understand how the app works
Needs to feel...
  • Not overwhelmed
  • Calm and reassured
  • Secure
Considerations
  • How could he register the pet in advance vs. need to do it at a time of crisis?

For each proto-character, we identified the key tasks as well as their emotional state at each stage of the journey.

User flows for each key moment helped us identify key actions within the app and the screens to focus on first

User flows

I mapped out the users’ steps to see how I could simplify their journey to help them reach their most important goals with the product.

Low fidelity wireframes help to understand and experience the defined flows
